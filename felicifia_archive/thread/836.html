Liberal Augustinianism: a new essay for Valentine's Day

by Elijah on 2013-02-11T20:20:00

To my mind, the unspeakable filth of sexuality is axiomatic. I am convinced that it is inherently wicked. It causes unimaginable, unbelievable, pervasive misery: from mild sexual frustration to rape-murder, it is (except bereavement) possibly the greatest cause of human emotional suffering. The question is what to do. (Note that this essay concerns humans exclusively.)

1. Is sexuality intrinsically bad?

Yes. I take it as axiomatic, again, that sexuality is bad independently of happiness and suffering. I do not know how to convince a skeptic, as with optimism versus pessimism. But even on wholly utilitarian grounds, sexuality is probably bad. As with opiates, the initial 'joy of sex' of youth fades, and sexuality becomes a chore - engaged in to satiate one's desires more than for pleasure. Moreover, on a negative utilitarian view, if the pleasure from sex is, say, 2n, and the suffering is n, it is still desirable to eliminate it.

2. What sexual actions are worst?

I believe that there are two dimensions of sexual evil. The first is utilitarian, the second is intrinsic. The second dimension concerns sexual conservatism or Puritanism, or that part of the evil of sexuality which is dissociable from the consequent suffering. In my mind, the least objectionable sexual practices on the second dimension are marriage-bed, missionary-position-type activities, and there is a gradient of abnormality and revulsion that includes masturbation, contraception, pornography, etc. The first dimension includes the consequent misery of sexual activity, encompassing sexual frustration, unrequited love, broken-heartedness, sexually transmitted disease, rape, etc. In my mind, the position of sexual activity on the first gradient is more important than the position on the second gradient. Therefore, the least objectionable sexual activities are those that involve little harm, including, say, masturbation - that is, activities that have little affective element. Of course, activities of this type generally include moderate wickedness on the second dimension.

3. What to do?

The 'final solution to the sexuality problem', of course, is eugenics (as for most other problems): that is, to use germ-line screening or 'designer genomes' to abolish this filthy aberration forever. Classical eugenic measures, such as to offer fecund parents money to be sterilized, are rather weaker, since they focus only on the extreme right tail of the sexuality bell curve. In the interim, a good technique is to provide immense amounts of pornographic material, as the Japanese do. Among young Japanese men, about one third claim that they are uninterested in sexual relationships, in part because their sexual desires are satiated by pornography. However, one runs the risk of producing extremely deviant, fetishistic material, as the Japanese have done (i.e., material that is wicked on the second gradient of sexual wickedness). A good solution to this problem is Orwellian in the most literal sense of the term: pornography is state-produced, available for a small cost, and any pornographic material from the private sector is punishable by life imprisonment, death, sterilization, exile, or other drastic measures. If one is a social conservative and wishes to focus on the second gradient of sexual wickedness (deviance), than one may instead try to ban contraception, pornography, etc. Thus 'liberal Augustinianism': the view that, while sexuality is wicked, its wickedness as a cause of suffering trumps its wickedness in itself.

One problem with the Japanese solution is that East Asians as a whole may be very K-selected. The notion that human races are r- or K-selected to varying degrees was originated by J. Philippe Rushton, and recently has been refined by a number of researchers. High K-selection indicates low sexual desire. Therefore, the pornography solution may not work. But it is worthy of investigation.
