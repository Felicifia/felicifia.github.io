Edited volume of David Pearce's works

by Elijah on 2013-05-29T02:25:00

David Pearce's contributions to utilitarianism are obviously extraordinarily significant. Therefore, we should honor his work in the form of an academically-published, dead-tree edited volume. (I'm thinking University of Buckingham Press might publish it: they have published a lot of politically incorrect and offbeat stuff, including a recent monograph on dysgenics and innovation rates, and two of Bruce Charlton's books.)

So here is what I propose.

Anyone who wants can help pitch in, and edit Dave's works. Copy and paste his essays into Word documents; add comments for Dave to review; correct typos; et cetera. Many of them contain citations in the form of embedded hyperlinks; these should be removed, and the citations converted to proper APA format. There is quite a bit of repetition throughout the essays (Dave has said he writes all his essays for someone discovering abolitionism for the first time); much of this should be removed. Where possible, short essays (e.g., Reprogramming Predators) should be integrated into the body of Hedonistic Imperative. (Some essays, such as WDAE, are very difficult to integrate because they are not germane to HI; others, such as Dave's review of Taking Animals Seriously, are too long to integrate.) Of course, be careful with how much you edit; don't tamper with that inimitable Pearcian spirit!

Moreover, this volume will ideally contain critical commentary on abolitionism, and Dave's response to this commentary. Some suggestions might be: the danger of extinction risk reduction; dysgenic trends and their relevance to transhumanism; criticism of negative utilitarianism; insect suffering; Bayesian, precautionary-principle, or some other approach to uncertain suffering; or practical suggestions, such as PR.

Thanks for reading! I hope you can help us out
