All Your Favorite EA Orgs are Recruiting!

by peterhurford on 2012-11-19T21:02:00

Rightly so, in my opinion, the effective altruism movement is expanding, and there are now tons of opportunities to get involved on the ground floor, if you sincerely think that's where your talents can be best served. Keeping in mind the general compellingness of "earning to give" to support these orgs in hiring rather than working for them directly and the effects of replacability, these orgs still need capable people to work their comparative advantage in these opportunities.

So what do we have?

Center for Effective Altruism
CEA is looking for a Head of Fundraising and External Relations to represent 80K, GWWC, EAA and the Life You Can Save. More info here (PDF) Direct questions to recruitment@centreforeffectivealtruism.org.

80,000 Hours
80K is looking for a Head of Career Advice and Research and Interns. See their recruitment page here. Direct questions to recruitment@centreforeffectivealtruism.org.

Giving What We Can
GWWC is looking for a Head of Communications and Interns. See their recruitment page here. Direct questions to recruitment@centreforeffectivealtruism.org.

Effective Animal Activism
EAA is looking for an Executive Director. See their recruitment page here. I hear from Eitan Fischer they will also accept interns. Direct questions to recruitment@centreforeffectivealtruism.org.

Wild Animal Suffering
Our very own Brian Tomasik says there could be full-time positions and internship opportunities with the upcoming non-profit working on wild animal suffering advocacy. Contact him for details and questions.

Other Nonhuman Animal Welfare Orgs
New Harvest is seeking a new director. The Humane Research Council is seeking a research / communications coordinator. The Farm Animal Rights Movement is seeking staff for the 10 billion lives tour.

The Life You Can Save
LYCS is looking for a Director of Outreach intern who gets to work with Peter Singer! Information here (PDF).

GiveWell
GiveWell is looking for full-time research analysts. See their recruitment page here. Direct questions to info@givewell.org.

Leverage Research
Leverage Research is looking for interns. See their recruitment page here. Direct questions to internship@leverageresearch.org.

by Brian Tomasik on 2012-11-20T05:55:00

By the way, if I may say so myself: The growth trajectory of EA is friggin' unbelievable! How did we get this far in just a few years?

Back in late 2006, there was no GiveWell, no LessWrong, no 80K. All we had was a blog called Overcoming Bias and a smaller blog called Felicifia. The latter became a forum in 2007 (?). The former became LessWrong in 2008 (?). Giving What We Can came along in 2009 and helped spark the Oxford contingent, leading to 80K, CEA, EAA, etc.

I still remember back in 2006 when I first met Toby Ord, he shared with me a small proposal he had circulated to friends about creating a group of people who would commit to donating to developing-world charities. It was actually Pablo who put me in touch with Toby, so thanks, Pablo! Incidentally, Pablo's website was the first thing I read when I Googled "utilitarianism" back in 2005, and on it I found Peter Singer's essays on animal suffering. Physics knows where I would be without it.
