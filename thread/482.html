Re: Question about QALYs and DALYs

by LadyMorgana on 2011-10-11T10:14:00

As RyanCarey indicates, 1 QALY is one year of life at full health (or equivalent) gained, whereas 1 DALY is one year of life at full health (or equivalent) lost. So we want to gain QALYs and avert DALYs.

I think that QALYs are the best measure from a utilitarian perspective, as the use of DALYs seems to always accompany the assumption that people live to 80 (and thus overemphasises the badness of people dying early in developing countries). I don't know the details though; PM Will Crouch (he's on Felicifa) if you want to know more.
